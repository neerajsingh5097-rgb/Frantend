A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Kalam played a crucial role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India". Kalam's contributions to India's defense and space programs were significant. He was involved in the development of ballistic missile technology and played a key role in the successful launch of India's first satellite, Aryabhata, in 1975. He also contributed to the development of the Agni and Prithvi missile systems. In addition to his scientific achievements, Kalam was known for his humility, dedication to education, and inspirational speeches. He served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was widely respected for his vision of a developed India and his efforts to promote education and scientific research. Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy continues to inspire millions of people in India and around the world.
